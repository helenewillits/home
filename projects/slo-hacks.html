SLO Hacks





SLO Hacks is a 36 hour hackathon hosted by MLH.


Our all-female team developed an app that allows users to increase their productivity by setting personal goals and betting against themselves and their friends. Users gain success points for every challenge that they win. The money that the user bet against themselves goes to charity for incomplete challenges.

Group Organization

I acted as a group organizer, planning what components needed to be implemented, what each member was working on, and what priorities our group needed to focus on. I also made sure to check in with each group member to help them stay on track and continue to make consistent progress. My perseverance in the final minutes leading up to the project presentation was a critical move in allowing our team to present a working product on the event floor.

Backend Design

I developed the backend for the android app using Java with Android Studio. I designed the stucture of the app, making sure to use proper cohesion when constructing classes and objects.

Frontend Design

I developed the Front End for features that allow users to choose a charity, view and modify their active challenges, and navigate between pages.

Obstacles

Working primarily on the backend of the app, I set out to use XPRING's XRP technology to allow for money transfers on the app. I encountered obstacles with implementing XRP due to the minimal documentation available on the technology with the gradle environment of Android Studio, as opposed to maven. Due to the time constraints of the hackathon, our team decided to focus on implementing other features of the app for presentation.
